Different Parts of the Cannabis Plant

These are the different parts of the cannabis plant explained.

PLANT PART 1

Cannabis Leaves

Cannabis leaves are the primary photosynthetic organs of the plant. While they contain minimal cannabinoids, they're often used for juicing and in some traditional preparations.

Primary photosynthetic organs
Used for juicing preparations
Minimal cannabinoid content
PLANT PART 2

Cannabis Flowers

The flowers or buds are the most valuable part of the cannabis plant, containing the highest concentration of cannabinoids like CBD and THC. These are primarily used for medicinal preparations and extracts.

Highest cannabinoid concentration
Used for medicinal extracts
Most valuable plant part
PLANT PART 3

Cannabis Seeds

Cannabis seeds are nutritionally rich and contain no psychoactive compounds. They're used for their protein content, healthy fats, and minerals. Seeds are generally legal in most jurisdictions.

Rich in protein and healthy fats
No psychoactive compounds
High nutritional value
PLANT PART 4

Cannabis Stems

The stems and stalks provide structural support and contain fiber that can be used for industrial purposes like textiles and construction materials. They have minimal cannabinoid content.

Key Compounds in Cannabis

Understanding CBD and THC - the primary cannabinoids

THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol)

THC is the primary psychoactive compound in cannabis that produces the characteristic "high" feeling. It binds to CB1 receptors in the brain and has been shown to help with pain management, appetite stimulation, and nausea reduction. THC is highly regulated in most countries but is approved for medical use in specific conditions.

Psychoactive Highly Regulated

CBD (Cannabidiol)

CBD is a calming, non-intoxicating compound found in cannabis that doesn't produce a "high" feeling. It has anti-inflammatory, anti-anxiety, and anticonvulsant properties. CBD is widely accepted for medical use and is legal in many jurisdictions when derived from hemp plants.

Myths vs Facts

Debunking common misconceptions about cannabis

MYTH

"Cannabis kills brain cells"

FACT

Research shows cannabis doesn't kill brain cells. Cannabinoids may even help protect and repair them.

MYTH

"All cannabis gets you high"

FACT

CBD and other cannabinoids don't cause a high. They provide therapeutic benefits without intoxication.

MYTH

"Higher THC means better quality"

FACT

Quality depends on terpenes and cannabinoid balance, not THC levels alone.

MYTH

"Cannabis use causes long-term memory loss"

FACT

Cannabis may affect short-term memory, but effects are temporary and reversible.

MYTH

"Cannabis causes cancer like tobacco"

FACT

Unlike tobacco, cannabis isn't clearly linked to cancer. Some studies suggest it may slow tumors.

MYTH

"You have to smoke cannabis to benefit"

FACT

Cannabis can be used as oils, edibles, tinctures, or topicals—smoking is just one method.
